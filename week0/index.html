Why Omelette?

  • Good Source Of Protein

    This is the first health benefit. Most of us seldom get enough protein when we eat a carbohydrate-rich grain based meal. Therefore, it helps if you eat an egg based breakfast.

  • Keeps You Full

    Carb-rich foods may fill you for one hour and may make you feel hungry soon. That might make you eat even more in your next meal. But when you eat a protein-rich meal, you tend to feel full for longer.

  • Other Nutriens

    Many vegetables can be added to the omelet as fillings. This way, you can derive more nutrients in the form of vitamins and minerals.

Contents

Ingredient Quantity
Egg 2
Butter 2 tsp
Salt 1/5 tsp
Black Pepper 1 dash

Cooking/Steps

  1. Beat eggs and pepper in small bowl until blended. (Adviced not to beat so much. You need to see white and yellow parts a little.)
  2. Heat butter in nonstick omelet pan over medium-high heat until hot. And add salt on it at last.
  3. Pour in egg mixture. And start slowly mixing from edges toward center with a spatula.(This is how reaches salty butter all over our omelette, and gives semi-smooth surface)
  4. Mix until you see cooked parts and a little liquid eggs on top. Wait for 20 seconds and turn it with spatula to make the liquid parts touch hot surface of pan.(You can divide two pairs to turn easily).
  5. When you turn it close the fire(heating) and take it after 1 minute.
  6. Serve immediately
